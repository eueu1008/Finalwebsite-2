Andy Warhol was born in Pittsburgh and moved to New York in 1949 after studying
design at the Carnegie Institute of Technology in Pittsburgh to become a commercial
artist. Andy Warhol soon became a successful and highly paid consumer advertise-
ment designer in New York. He then switched his career to creating art for galleries
and became a representative artist of the 1960s pop art movement. Pop art was
inspired by the imagery of popular culture, which celebrated and criticized consum-
ption choices and mass production. Andy Warhol worked in a wide range of media
such as printmaking, painting, hand drawing, performance art, silk screening, sculp-
ture, photography, music and film. Warhols most well known artworks are images of
soup cans, soda bottles, dollar bills, and iconic paintings of Mick Jagger, Elvis Pres-
ley, Marilyn Monroe and many more. After his fame reached high, he built a studio
called The Factory on 47th street. His goal as an artist was to bring popular styles
and subjects into the world of high class art.
